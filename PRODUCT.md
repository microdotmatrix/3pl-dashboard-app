# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Ops management and warehouse staff at Honeybee Herb (4-5 people total). Two super-admins handle billing, user management, and ShipStation sync oversight. Two to three staff users monitor shipment queues and log whiteboard notes. They work at laptops in office or warehouse environments during normal business hours — well-lit, task-focused, not emergency-response intensity.

## Product Purpose

Internal operations dashboard for GreenBox 3PL, Honeybee Herb's boutique third-party logistics service for cannabis accessory brands. The tool covers the full ops loop: real-time shipment monitoring synced from ShipStation, team whiteboard for shared notes and communication, month-end billing reports by client, and user/access management. Success means the team can check statuses, review billing, and coordinate without switching between tools or fighting the UI.

## Brand Personality

Precise, fast, modern. GreenBox 3PL's identity is grounded in the brand: forest green, geometric, clean. The tool should feel like it was built by people who care about their work — not assembled from a component library and shipped. Voice is direct and operational; no filler copy, no friendly-robot tone.

## References

The GreenBox 3PL marketing site (greenbox3pl.com) is the visual anchor: light mint background, vivid forest green primary, clean modern sans-serif, generous whitespace without feeling sparse. The logo — a faceted 3D cube in forest-to-medium green — is the identity origin. The internal tool should feel like it belongs to the same brand family, not like an unrelated admin panel.

## Anti-references

AI-generated SaaS slop: hero metrics with gradient accents, identical card grids, glassmorphism, gradient text clips, side-stripe border callouts, modal-first interactions, meaningless skeleton loaders. These patterns signal that no one made a real decision. Avoid anything a viewer could immediately identify as AI-generated or component-library-default.

## Design Principles

1. **Density earns trust.** Ops tools are judged by information density. Show the data clearly rather than hiding it behind progressive disclosure or empty states that feel patronizing to experienced users.
2. **Brand coherence across surfaces.** The internal tool should be recognizable as the same family as the marketing site — same green system, same restraint, same confidence.
3. **Speed over ceremony.** Zero friction to find a shipment, check a status, or log a note. No confirmation dialogs for reads, no tours, no onboarding theater.
4. **Earned color.** The forest green identity is strong; use it with intention. Neutrals carry the layout; color signals status and primary action.
5. **No performance theater.** Animations carry meaning or don't exist. No pulse badges for decorative effect, no animated counters, no bounce.

## Theme

Both light and dark modes. Default to the user's OS preference. Selectable via theme toggle in the app. Selection persists to the user's profile. Light theme draws from the marketing site's mint-and-forest-green palette; dark theme uses deeply tinted neutrals anchored to the same green hue.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Small team of known users, no specific stated accommodations. Ensure sufficient contrast in both light and dark themes, particularly for status badges (pending/approved/suspended) and chart colors.
